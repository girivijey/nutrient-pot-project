How Nutripot Works — Lifecycle
1
Sowing
DAY 0
Fill Nutripot with coco peat or soil mix. Sow 1–2 seeds per pot. Keep in shaded nursery area and water lightly.
2
Germination
DAY 7–10
Roots grow freely and can penetrate through pot walls directly. No barrier to root expansion — natural growth continues.
3
Transplanting
DAY 21–28
Plant the entire pot directly into the field or grow bag. No need to remove the pot. Root system stays completely undisturbed.
4
Pot Degradation
DAY 30–45
Pot breaks down in soil. Coir, vermicompost, neem cake, rock phosphate, and banana peel powder release nutrients directly to roots.
5
Full Establishment
DAY 45+
Plant thrives on slow-release starter nutrients from degraded pot. Strong roots, healthy growth, no plastic residue left behind.

Farmer's Guide
👨‍🌾 Step-by-Step Usage

Step 1: Fill Nutripot with coco peat or nursery soil mix.
Step 2: Sow 1–2 seeds and cover lightly. Water gently.
Step 3: Keep in partial shade for 3–5 days after germination.
Step 4: After 3–4 weeks, transplant the entire pot to field.
Step 5: Water normally — pot dissolves completely in 30–45 days.

✅ Key Benefits

✔ No transplant shock — roots stay undisturbed
✔ Neem cake protects from soil pests & fungi naturally
✔ Rock phosphate builds a strong root system
✔ Vermicompost feeds early growth — no extra fertiliser needed
✔ Fully biodegradable — zero plastic waste in the field
✔ Saves labour — no pot removal at transplanting time
✔ Reduces water stress during establishment phase

⚠ Storage & Handling Tips

· Store pots in a cool, dry place away from direct rain.
· Do not soak in water before use — fill gently.
· Use within 6 months of manufacture for best nutrient activity.
· Handle with care — walls are firm but can crack if dropped.
